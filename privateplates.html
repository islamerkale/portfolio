Project Overview

Private Plates Vancouver, a premium private chef service, needed a digital platform to showcase their services for busy families and special events. The goal was to create an elegant website that reflects their culinary expertise while making it easy for customers to book services.

Key Challenges & Solutions

Service Differentiation

Private Plates offers three distinct services (Weekly Meal Prep, Weddings & Events, Private Dining) that needed clear differentiation while maintaining a cohesive brand identity. Customers were confused about which service suited their needs.

Without clear service distinction, potential customers were dropping off without booking.

Visual Service Cards

Created distinct visual cards for each service with unique icons, color coding, and clear value propositions. Implemented a service selection wizard in the booking form to guide users to the right service.

Each service card includes specific features and a clear call-to-action tailored to that service type.

Mobile Booking Experience

With 75% of users accessing the site on mobile devices, the complex booking form with dietary preferences, scheduling, and service options needed to be simplified for mobile without losing functionality.

The original form had high abandonment rates on mobile due to complexity and poor UX.

Progressive Mobile Form

Implemented a multi-step form with progress indicators, touch-friendly checkboxes, and conditional logic that shows/hides fields based on previous selections. Used native mobile inputs for dates and times.

The form adapts to mobile screen sizes while maintaining all necessary functionality for complete service requests.

Design Process

1

Research & Discovery

Conducted user interviews with busy families and event planners in Vancouver to understand their needs, pain points, and decision-making process when hiring private chef services. Analyzed competitor websites and booking flows.

"Vancouver customers value transparency in pricing, clear service differentiation, and mobile-friendly booking. They need to quickly understand which service fits their specific situation."

2

Information Architecture

Structured the website to prioritize service discovery with clear pathways to booking. Key sections include Services, Process, Philosophy, Testimonials, and Contact. Created user flows for each service type.

Information architecture showing service-focused structure

3

Wireframing & Prototyping

Developed low-fidelity wireframes focusing on service differentiation and mobile optimization. Created interactive prototypes to test the booking flow and service selection process with potential users.

4

Visual Design & Testing

Implemented a sophisticated visual design reflecting the premium nature of private chef services. Used food photography, elegant typography, and a warm color palette. Conducted usability testing with Vancouver residents to refine the booking experience.

Final design mockups showing desktop and mobile interfaces
